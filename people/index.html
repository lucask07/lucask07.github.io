Student Researchers

Abraham Stroschein

Abe graduated summa cum laude from St. Thomas in Spring 2024 with dual majors in Computer Engineering and Physics. In the research lab, Abe designed and developed Python code for the COVG amplifier project.

Seth Lindgren

Seth is a St. Thomas Electrical Engineering student with an interest in physics. In the research lab, he has developed a new time-of-flight experimental system which we are using to explore unconventional applications of timing light.

Thomas Gagnon

Thomas is a St. Thomas Computer Engineering student who is pursuing a double major in physics. In the research lab, he has been implementing image processing algorithms onto Opal Kelly FPGAs.

Ian Delgadillo Bonequi

Ian graduated from St. Thomas summa cum laude with a degree in Electrical Engineering and a minor in Physics (Spring 2023). Ian is now an FPGA engineer at General Dynamics. In the lab, he developed Verilog code and advanced control systems design for the COVG amplifier project.

Jonathan Millam

Jon is a St. Thomas Computer Engineering student. In the research lab, Jon has investigated material classfication using time-of-flight transients. Jon loves to code and is the lab PyTorch machine learning expert.

Jordan Haag

Jordan is a UST electrical engineering major and Air Force ROTC student. He loves PCB design and test and has contributed two PCB designs to the COVG amplifier project. He is a recipient of an Air Force graduate program scholarship to continue his electrical engineering studies after UST.

Kordt Gammelgaard

Kordt Gammelgaard is a UST EE major with an interest in physics. In the summer of 2023, Kordt received a UROP Young scholars grant to explore material classification with time-of-flight sensors.

Cienna Becker

Cienna is a student at St. Thomas undergraduate graduating in the Spring of 2024 with a degree in Computer Engineering and multiple minors! During the summer of 2022 she demonstrated successful classification of different types of plastics using a time-of-flight depth sensor (AMS TMF8801) funded by a UROP Young Scholars grant.

Jakob Porter

Jake Porter is a senior undergraduate student majoring in Electrical Engineering. He contributed to the analog and PCB design aspects of the NIH COVG project. He is diving into switch mode DC/DC converters and analog feedback loops, while also learning KiCAD.

Corissa Uberecken

Corissa is a fourth-year student majoring in Computer Engineering (graduating Spring 2024) who completed a term studying abroad in Australia. In the research lab during the summer of 2021 she became the group expert on the Texas Instruments ADS8686 ADC and brought up a development board using the part.
